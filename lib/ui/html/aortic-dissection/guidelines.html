Screening for Aortic Dissection

Presenting symptoms:

  • Acute, severe chest, back, or abdominal pain
  • Numbness or weakness in any limbs
  • Syncope

Patient Risk Factors:

  • PMH: HTN, Smoker
  • Known aortic aneurysm
  • Family Hx of dissection
  • H/o Cocaine use
  • Recent aortic manipulation (aortic surgery, stenting, angiography, etc.)
  • Connective tissue disorders (Marfan, Loeys-Dietz, Ehlers-Danlos, Turner syndromes)
  • Bicuspid aortic valve

Clinical Findings:

  • Pulse deficit (radial and/or femoral)
  • Blood pressure discrepancy between limbs (systolic BP difference > 20 mmHg)
  • Signs of stroke or paraplegia
  • New heart murmur of aortic insufficiency
  • Abdominal tenderness, bloody stool
  • Oliguria / Anuria

Diagnostic testing:

  • CTA chest/abdomen/pelvis without and with contrast (Use ECG-gating, if available)
    • No oral contrast is necessary
    • Look for secondary findings: Pericardial effusion, organ malperfusion
  • 12 lead ECG (signs of myocardial ischemia / ST elevation MI)
  • Labs (Lactate, D-dimer, CBC, BMP, LFTs, Coag panel, Type & Cross)

Initial Treatment:

  • Keep NPO
  • Large bore IV access x 2
  • Continuous heart rate and BP monitor
  • Arterial line (if possible, do not delay transfer to insert)
  • Reverse coagulopathy if anticoagulated
  • Maintain Systolic BP between 100-120 mmHg and HR < 60
    • First Line anti-impulse therapy with IV betablockers: Esmolol or Labetalol infusion
    • If Beta-blocker contraindicated: Diltiazem infusion
    • For secondary BP control (once HR < 60): Nitroprusside or Clevidipine or Nicardipine infusion
  • Pain control: Morphine prn or Dilaudid prn
